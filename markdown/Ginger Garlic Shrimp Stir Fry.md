Ginger-Garlic Shrimp Stir-Fry
A fast weeknight stir-fry with anti-inflammatory ginger, garlic, and colorful vegetables — on the table in 20 minutes.

Servings: 4
Total Time: 20 minutes

INGREDIENTS
• 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
• 2 cups broccoli florets
• 1 red bell pepper, sliced
• 1 cup snap peas, trimmed
• 1 large carrot, thinly sliced
• 4 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
• 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
• 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
• 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
• 1 teaspoon sesame oil
• 1 teaspoon cornstarch
• 2 tablespoons water
• 0.25 teaspoon red pepper flakes
• 2 green onions, sliced
• 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
• Quinoa, for serving

STEPS
1. Make the sauce: Whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, cornstarch, and water in a small bowl. Set aside.
2. Cook the shrimp: Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook 1-2 minutes per side until pink and just cooked through. Remove and set aside.
3. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil to the pan. Add carrot and broccoli, stir-fry 2 minutes. Add bell pepper and snap peas, stir-fry another 2 minutes until tender-crisp. Add garlic, ginger, and red pepper flakes, cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Combine: Return shrimp to the pan. Pour the sauce over everything and toss to coat. Cook 1 minute until the sauce thickens and glazes the shrimp and vegetables.
5. Serve over quinoa, topped with green onions and sesame seeds.
