Spiced Lentils with Carrots
A saucy one-skillet green lentil main warm with cumin, ginger, cinnamon, and allspice, lightly sweetened by carrots and dried fruit. Source: budgetbytes.com (4.86 stars, 99 votes).

Servings: 4
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes

INGREDIENTS
• 1 yellow onion, diced
• 4 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
• 4 carrots, peeled and diced (about 2 cups)
• 2 tablespoons olive oil
• 1 teaspoon ground cumin
• 0.5 teaspoon ground cinnamon
• 0.5 teaspoon ground allspice
• 0.25 teaspoon crushed red pepper, optional
• 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
• 3 tablespoons tomato paste
• 0.25 cup chopped dried apricots or raisins
• 3 cups vegetable broth
• 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped, optional

STEPS
1. Dice the onion, mince the garlic, grate the ginger, and peel and dice the carrots.
2. Add the onion, garlic, ginger, and olive oil to a deep skillet. Sauté over medium heat about 5 minutes until the onions are soft and translucent.
3. Add the carrots, cumin, cinnamon, allspice, and crushed red pepper (if using). Sauté about 2 minutes more.
4. Add the lentils, tomato paste, dried apricots or raisins, and broth. Stir until the tomato paste dissolves. Cover and bring to a boil over high heat.
5. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, covered, about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils and carrots are very tender.
6. Taste and season with salt and pepper as needed. Serve over rice, polenta, or mashed potatoes and top with parsley.

NOTES
Omit the crushed red pepper for a toddler-friendly pot and add a pinch to individual bowls instead. Stir in a few handfuls of baby spinach or kale at the end for extra greens. Use green or brown lentils (not red), which hold their shape. Keeps well refrigerated; thin with a splash of broth when reheating.
