Slow Cooker Lentil and Vegetable Curry
A hands-off, deeply spiced lentil curry loaded with anti-inflammatory turmeric, ginger, and colorful vegetables — dump it in by noon, eat by six.

Servings: 6
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 6 hours

INGREDIENTS
• 1.5 cups dried red lentils, rinsed
• 1 can coconut milk (14 oz, full fat)
• 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
• 2 cups vegetable broth
• 1 large sweet potato, peeled and cubed
• 2 cups baby spinach
• 1 yellow onion, diced
• 4 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
• 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
• 2 teaspoons ground turmeric
• 1.5 teaspoons ground cumin
• 1 teaspoon ground coriander
• 0.5 teaspoon cinnamon
• 0.25 teaspoon cayenne pepper
• 1 teaspoon salt
• 0.5 teaspoon black pepper
• 1 lime, juiced
• Fresh cilantro, for garnish
• Quinoa, for serving

STEPS
1. Sauté the aromatics: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add garlic, ginger, turmeric, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cayenne. Stir for 1 minute until fragrant. Transfer to the slow cooker.
2. Add the base: Add rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes, coconut milk, vegetable broth, sweet potato, salt, and black pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
3. Cook low and slow: Cover and cook on low for 6 hours or high for 3 hours, until lentils are tender and sweet potato is soft.
4. Finish: Stir in baby spinach and lime juice. Let sit 5 minutes until spinach wilts. Taste and adjust salt.
5. Serve over quinoa, topped with fresh cilantro.
