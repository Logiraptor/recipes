Spicy mango shrimp chopped salad
Chili-lime shrimp, sweet mango, creamy avocado, and a zippy ginger-lime dressing. The brightest salad of the week — comes together in under 15 minutes.

INGREDIENTS
• 0.8 pounds large shrimp, peeled and deveined
• 1 cups mango, diced
• 1 cups romaine lettuce, chopped
• 1 cups red cabbage, shredded
• 1 avocado, diced
• 0.5 cups shelled edamame
• 0.3 cups red onion, finely minced
• 1 jalapeño, minced (optional)
• 0.3 cups fresh cilantro, chopped
• 2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds
• 1 teaspoons chili powder
• 1 teaspoons garlic powder
• 0.5 teaspoons salt
• 1 tablespoons olive oil (for cooking shrimp)
• 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
• 4 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
• 1 tablespoons rice vinegar
• 1 teaspoons fresh ginger, grated
• 1 teaspoons honey
• 0.3 teaspoons chili flakes

STEPS
1. Make the ginger-lime dressing: Whisk together 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, 4 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons), 1 tablespoons rice vinegar, 1 teaspoons fresh ginger, grated, 1 teaspoons honey, and 0.3 teaspoons chili flakes in a small bowl or jar. Set aside.
2. Season the shrimp: Pat 0.8 pounds large shrimp, peeled and deveined dry with a paper towel. Toss with 1 teaspoons chili powder, 1 teaspoons garlic powder, and 0.5 teaspoons salt until evenly coated.
3. Cook the shrimp: Heat 1 tablespoons olive oil (for cooking shrimp) in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook 1–2 minutes per side until pink and just curled. Do not crowd the pan. Remove from heat immediately.
4. Build the salad base: In a large bowl, combine 1 cups romaine lettuce, chopped, 1 cups red cabbage, shredded, 0.5 cups shelled edamame, 0.3 cups red onion, finely minced, 1 cups mango, diced, 0.3 cups fresh cilantro, chopped, and 1 jalapeño, minced (optional) if using. Toss to combine.
5. Assemble and serve: Add shrimp to the salad. Add 1 avocado, diced last. Drizzle dressing over everything, toss gently, and finish with 2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds. Serve right away.

NOTES
Don't overcook the shrimp — they go from perfect to rubbery fast. Pull them off heat the moment they curl into a C shape and turn pink. Frozen shrimp work great here; thaw in a bowl of cold water for 10 minutes. Dressing stores 5 days refrigerated. Skip the jalapeño for a milder version.
