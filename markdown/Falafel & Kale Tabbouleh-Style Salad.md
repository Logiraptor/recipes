Falafel & kale tabbouleh-style salad
A tabbouleh-meets-grain-bowl mashup loaded with herbs, farro, and crispy falafel. Fully plant-based and packed with fiber, folate, and gut-friendly goodness.

INGREDIENTS
• 6 falafel (store-bought or homemade)
• 1 cups cooked farro or quinoa
• 2 cups lacinato (dinosaur) kale, finely chopped
• 1 cups flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
• 0.5 cups fresh mint, finely chopped
• 0.5 cups tomatoes, diced
• 0.5 cups cucumber, diced
• 0.3 cups pomegranate seeds
• 0.3 cups red onion, finely minced
• 2 tablespoons hemp seeds
• 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
• 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 1½ lemons)
• 1 garlic clove, finely grated
• 0.3 teaspoons ground allspice
• 0.5 teaspoons salt

STEPS
1. Massage the kale: Place 2 cups lacinato (dinosaur) kale, finely chopped in a large bowl. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt and add a squeeze of lemon. Using your hands, massage the kale firmly for about 1–2 minutes until it softens and darkens. This removes bitterness and helps it hold up for days.
2. Make the dressing: Whisk together 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 1½ lemons), 1 garlic clove, finely grated, 0.3 teaspoons ground allspice, and 0.5 teaspoons salt in a small bowl until combined. Taste — it should be bright and lemony.
3. Build the salad: To the bowl with kale, add 1 cups cooked farro or quinoa, 1 cups flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped, 0.5 cups fresh mint, finely chopped, 0.5 cups cherry tomatoes, halved, 0.5 cups cucumber, diced, 0.3 cups pomegranate seeds, and 0.3 cups red onion, finely minced. Pour dressing over and toss well. Sprinkle in 2 tablespoons hemp seeds and toss again.
4. Warm the falafel and serve: If using store-bought falafel, warm in a 375°F oven or air fryer for 8–10 minutes until crispy. Halve or leave whole and arrange on top of the salad. Serve immediately with falafel on top.

NOTES
Falafel can be made in large batches and frozen — reheat in a 375°F oven for 10 minutes straight from frozen. For the kale: massaging it is the secret to a salad that holds well without getting tough. This salad actually improves after a few hours in the fridge as the grains soak up the dressing. Store falafel separately so they stay crisp.
