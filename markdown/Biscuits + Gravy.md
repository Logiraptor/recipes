Biscuits and Sausage Gravy
Soft, buttery biscuits smothered in creamy sausage gravy — the ultimate Southern comfort breakfast.

Servings: 6

INGREDIENTS
• 2 cups all-purpose flour (for biscuits)
• 1 tablespoons baking powder
• 0.5 teaspoons salt (for biscuits)
• 6 tablespoons cold butter, cut into small cubes
• 0.8 cups cold buttermilk (or milk with 1 tsp vinegar)
• 1 pounds breakfast pork sausage (mild)
• 0.3 cups all-purpose flour (for gravy)
• 2.5 cups whole milk
• 0.5 teaspoons salt (for gravy)
• 1 teaspoons black pepper (for gravy — be generous)

STEPS
1. Make the biscuit dough: Preheat oven to 425°F. Mix 2 cups all-purpose flour (for biscuits), 1 tablespoons baking powder, and 0.5 teaspoons salt (for biscuits). Work 6 tablespoons cold butter, cut into small cubes into the flour with your fingers until crumbly. Stir in 0.8 cups cold buttermilk (or milk with 1 tsp vinegar) until just combined. Do not overwork.
2. Shape and bake biscuits: Turn dough out on a floured surface. Pat to 1-inch thick. Cut into rounds or squares and place on a baking sheet. Bake 12–14 min until golden brown.
3. Brown the sausage: While biscuits bake, cook 1 pounds breakfast pork sausage (mild) in a large skillet over medium heat, breaking it up, until browned throughout.
4. Make the gravy: Sprinkle 0.3 cups all-purpose flour (for gravy) over the sausage and stir to coat. Cook 1–2 min. Gradually pour in 2.5 cups whole milk, stirring constantly to prevent lumps.
5. Thicken the gravy: Simmer over medium heat, stirring often, until thick and creamy, about 4–5 min. Season generously with 0.5 teaspoons salt (for gravy) and 1 teaspoons black pepper (for gravy — be generous).
6. Serve: Split biscuits and ladle gravy over the top.

NOTES
Toddler tip: tear the biscuit into small pieces and serve with a small amount of mild gravy on the side for dipping. Biscuits freeze well — make a double batch and freeze half for future mornings. Reheat in the microwave 30–45 seconds.
