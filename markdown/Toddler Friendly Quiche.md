Toddler-Friendly Veggie Quiche
A mild, creamy quiche packed with hidden veggies — perfect for little ones and grown-ups alike!

INGREDIENTS
• 1 store-bought pie crust (9-inch)
• 4 large eggs
• 0.8 cups whole milk
• 0.3 cups heavy cream
• 1 cups shredded mild cheddar cheese
• 0.5 cups frozen chopped spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
• 0.5 cups small broccoli florets, finely chopped
• 1 tablespoons butter
• 0.3 teaspoons salt
• 0.1 teaspoons garlic powder

STEPS
1. Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Press the pie crust into a 9-inch pie dish and crimp the edges. Prick the bottom a few times with a fork.
2. Blind bake the crust: Line the crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 10 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment and bake for another 5 minutes until lightly golden. Set aside.
3. Sauté the veggies: Melt 1 tablespoons butter in a small pan over medium heat. Add the chopped broccoli and cook for 3–4 minutes until tender. Remove from heat and stir in the spinach.
4. Make the egg mixture: In a bowl, whisk together 4 large eggs, 0.8 cups whole milk, 0.3 cups heavy cream, 0.3 teaspoons salt, and 0.1 teaspoons garlic powder until smooth and well combined.
5. Assemble: Spread the sautéed veggies evenly over the pre-baked crust. Sprinkle 1 cups shredded mild cheddar cheese over the top, then pour the egg mixture over everything.
6. Bake: Bake at 375°F for 30–35 minutes, until the center is just set and the top is lightly golden. A knife inserted in the center should come out clean.
7. Cool and serve: Let the quiche cool for at least 10 minutes before slicing — this helps it hold together and also brings it to a toddler-safe temperature. Cut into small wedges or squares for little hands!

NOTES
**Toddler tips:**
- **Mild flavors**: This recipe avoids onions, strong spices, or salty meats that toddlers often reject.
- **Hidden veggies**: The spinach blends right into the egg custard — they'll never notice!
- **Make it fices into stars or hearts.
- **Swap ideas**: Try diced sweet potato, peas, or corn in place of the broccoli.
- **Freezer-friendly**: Slice and freeze individual portions for quick toddler meals during the week.
