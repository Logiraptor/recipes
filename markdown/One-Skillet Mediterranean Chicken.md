One-Skillet Mediterranean Chicken
Seared chicken braised in a garlicky lemon, white wine, tomato, and olive sauce with oregano and parsley. Source: themediterraneandish.com (5.0 stars, 88 votes).

Servings: 4
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes

INGREDIENTS
• 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts (thighs also work)
• 2 tablespoons garlic, minced
• Kosher salt
• Black pepper
• 1 tablespoon dried oregano, divided
• 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
• 0.5 cup dry white wine (or extra chicken broth)
• 1 large lemon, juiced
• 0.5 cup chicken broth
• 1 medium red onion, finely chopped
• 4 small tomatoes, diced (or 1 can chopped tomatoes, 15 oz)
• 0.25 cup sliced green olives (or kalamata, or capers)
• 1 handful fresh parsley, chopped
• Crumbled feta cheese, optional

STEPS
1. Pat the chicken dry. Make three shallow slits on each side of each breast.
2. Rub garlic over both sides, pushing some into the slits. Season both sides with salt, pepper, and half the oregano.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Sear chicken on both sides, then add white wine and reduce by half.
4. Add lemon juice and chicken broth. Sprinkle on the remaining oregano. Reduce heat to medium, cover, and cook about 5-6 minutes per side until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
5. Uncover; top with chopped onion, tomatoes, and olives. Cover again and cook 3 minutes.
6. Finish with parsley and feta, if using. Serve over rice or orzo, or with crusty bread.

NOTES
Add a handful of spinach just before serving for extra greens. Thighs work in place of breast.
