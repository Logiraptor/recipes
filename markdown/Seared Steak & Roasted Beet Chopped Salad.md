Seared steak & roasted beet chopped salad
Earthy roasted beets, peppery greens, and thin-sliced grass-fed steak — dressed with a balsamic-walnut vinaigrette. The one indulgent salad of the week, and worth every bite.

INGREDIENTS
• 12 ounces flank or sirloin steak (grass-fed preferred)
• 2 small beets, roasted and cubed
• 2 cups arugula
• 1 cups radicchio, shredded
• 0.3 cups walnuts, roughly chopped
• 0.3 cups goat cheese or feta, crumbled
• 0.3 cups flat-leaf parsley
• 0.3 cups red onion, thinly sliced
• 0.5 cups radishes, thinly sliced
• 1 tablespoons olive oil (for the steak)
• 1 teaspoons salt and black pepper
• 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
• 1 tablespoons walnut or extra-virgin olive oil
• 1 teaspoons honey
• 1 teaspoons Dijon mustard
• 1 pinch black pepper

STEPS
1. Roast the beets: Preheat oven to 400°F. Wrap 2 small beets, roasted and cubed individually in foil and roast for 45–50 minutes until a knife slides through easily. Let cool, peel, and cube. (Can be done 1–5 days ahead.)
2. Make the dressing: Whisk together 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar, 1 tablespoons walnut or extra-virgin olive oil, 1 teaspoons honey, 1 teaspoons Dijon mustard, and 1 pinch black pepper in a small jar until emulsified. Taste and adjust — it should be tangy with a hint of sweetness.
3. Sear the steak: Pat 12 ounces flank or sirloin steak (grass-fed preferred) dry and season generously with 1 teaspoons salt and black pepper. Heat a cast-iron or heavy skillet over high heat until smoking. Add 1 tablespoons olive oil (for the steak) and sear steak 3–4 min per side for medium-rare. Remove and rest on a cutting board.
4. Rest and slice: Rest steak for at least 5 minutes — this is non-negotiable for juicy results. Slice thinly against the grain.
5. Assemble and serve: In a large bowl, combine 2 cups arugula, 1 cups radicchio, shredded, 0.3 cups red onion, thinly sliced, 0.5 cups radishes, thinly sliced, and 0.3 cups flat-leaf parsley. Add roasted 2 small beets, roasted and cubed and 0.3 cups walnuts, roughly chopped. Arrange steak slices on top. Scatter 0.3 cups goat cheese or feta, crumbled over everything, drizzle with dressing, and serve.

NOTES
Roast beets ahead: wrap in foil at 400°F for 45–50 min, then refrigerate up to 5 days. Store sliced steak separately from the salad base — it holds 3 days. For medium-rare, pull the steak off heat at 130°F internal and let it rise to 135°F while resting.
