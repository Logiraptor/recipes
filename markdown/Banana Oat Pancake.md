Banana Oat Pancakes with Maple Syrup
Naturally sweet, fluffy, and made with oats for fiber — a weekday breakfast the whole family will love.

INGREDIENTS
• 1 pieces ripe banana, mashed
• 1 cups rolled oats
• 1 cups all-purpose flour
• 2 pieces large eggs
• 1 cups milk
• 2 teaspoons baking powder
• 0.5 teaspoons cinnamon
• 1 teaspoons vanilla extract
• 2 tablespoons maple syrup (plus more for serving)
• 1 tablespoons butter or cooking spray for pan

STEPS
1. Mix wet ingredients: In a large bowl, mix 1 pieces ripe banana, mashed, 2 pieces large eggs, 1 cups milk, 1 teaspoons vanilla extract, and 2 tablespoons maple syrup (plus more for serving) until combined.
2. Add dry ingredients: Add 1 cups rolled oats, 1 cups all-purpose flour, 2 teaspoons baking powder, and 0.5 teaspoons cinnamon to the wet ingredients. Stir until just combined — a few lumps are fine. Let rest 5 min.
3. Cook the pancakes: Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add a small amount of 1 tablespoons butter or cooking spray for pan. Pour about 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and edges look set, about 2–3 min. Flip and cook another 1–2 min.
4. Serve: Serve warm with maple syrup.

NOTES
Batter can be made the night before and stored in the fridge. Leftover pancakes reheat in a toaster in 1–2 min. Toddler tip: cut into small strips — perfect for little hands. Add blueberries or mini chocolate chips to the batter if desired.
