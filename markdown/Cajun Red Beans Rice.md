Cajun Red Beans & Rice
A flavorful, heart-healthy, egg-free, toddler-friendly Cajun classic — designed for batch cooking and big flavor without too much heat.

Servings: 6

INGREDIENTS
• 1.5 cups rice
• 3 pieces bacon, chopped
• 12 ounces smoked sausage, sliced into half-moons
• 2 cans red kidney beans, drained and rinsed
• 1 tablespoon olive oil
• 1 small onion, diced
• 1 green bell pepper, diced
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning (low sodium)
• 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
• 0.25 teaspoons black pepper
• 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
• Parsley for garnish

STEPS
1. Cook the rice.
2. Cook the bacon in a dutch oven until cooked but not crispy.
2. Cook the veggies and sausage: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium heat. Add 1 small onion, diced, 1 green bell pepper, diced. Sauté 5–7 minutes until soft. Add 2 garlic cloves, minced and cook 1 minute more. Stir in sliced sausage and cook until lightly browned, about 3–4 minutes.
3. Simmer the beans: Add 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, 0.5 teaspoons dried thyme, 1 teaspoon smoked paprika, 0.25 teaspoons black pepper, and 1 tablespoon tomato paste. Stir well. Add 2 cans red kidney beans, drained and rinsed, 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth, and 1 teaspoon soy sauce. Bring to a simmer. Reduce heat, cover partially, and let simmer 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally. If too watery, remove lid and simmer uncovered last 5–10 min until thickened.
4. Serve: Spoon bean mixture over cooked rice. Garnish with green onions or parsley if you like.

NOTES
DIY Cajun seasoning: mix 1 tsp paprika, ½ tsp garlic powder, ½ tsp onion powder, ½ tsp thyme, ¼ tsp black pepper, and optionally ¼ tsp cayenne (skip for toddlers). Toddler tip: the turkey sausage is milder than andouille and the beans are soft and easy for little hands. Reheats well — add a splash of broth if it thickens too much.
