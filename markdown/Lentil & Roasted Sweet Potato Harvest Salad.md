Lentil & roasted sweet potato harvest salad
A hearty autumn-inspired salad with caramelized sweet potato, protein-rich French lentils, crisp apple, and toasted pecans — all tied together with a tangy apple cider-maple dressing. Comforting and completely plant-based.

INGREDIENTS
• 1 cups French lentils, cooked
• 1 medium sweet potato, cubed
• 2 cups arugula
• 1 cups Brussels sprouts, raw and thinly shredded
• 1 apple (Honeycrisp or Granny Smith), diced
• 0.3 cups dried cranberries (unsweetened)
• 0.3 cups walnuts, toasted
• 0.3 cups goat cheese, crumbled
• 0.3 cups red onion, thinly sliced
• 1 tablespoons olive oil (for roasting)
• 0.5 teaspoons salt and pepper (for roasting)
• 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
• 1 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
• 1 teaspoons pure maple syrup
• 1 teaspoons Dijon mustard
• 0.3 teaspoons ground cinnamon
• 0.3 teaspoons salt and pepper (for dressing)

STEPS
1. Roast the sweet potato: Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss cubed 1 medium sweet potato, cubed with 1 tablespoons olive oil (for roasting) and 0.5 teaspoons salt and pepper (for roasting). Spread on a baking sheet in a single layer and roast 22–25 minutes, flipping once halfway, until caramelized at the edges.
2. Make the apple cider-maple dressing: While the sweet potato roasts, whisk together 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, 1 tablespoons apple cider vinegar, 1 teaspoons pure maple syrup, 1 teaspoons Dijon mustard, 0.3 teaspoons ground cinnamon, and 0.3 teaspoons salt and pepper (for dressing) in a small bowl until smooth and emulsified.
3. Toast the nuts: Toast 0.3 cups pecans or walnuts, toasted in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3–4 minutes, stirring often, until fragrant and lightly golden. Watch carefully — they go from toasted to burnt quickly. Let cool.
4. Build the salad: In a large bowl, combine 2 cups arugula, 1 cups Brussels sprouts, raw and thinly shredded, 0.3 cups red onion, thinly sliced, and 1 cups French lentils, cooked. Add the warm roasted 1 medium sweet potato, cubed and 1 apple (Honeycrisp or Granny Smith), diced. Toss gently.
5. Dress and finish: Drizzle dressing over the salad and toss to coat. Top with 0.3 cups walnuts, toasted, 0.3 cups dried cranberries (unsweetened), and 0.3 cups goat cheese, crumbled. Serve immediately or let sit 10 minutes for the greens to soften slightly.

NOTES
Cook a double batch of French lentils on Sunday — they're used in two recipes this week and keep 5 days refrigerated. Roasted sweet potato also holds 5 days. This salad benefits from sitting 10–15 minutes after dressing; the arugula wilts slightly and the flavors meld beautifully. For extra heartiness, add a soft-boiled egg.
