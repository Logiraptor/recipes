Red Beans and Rice with Andouille Sausage
A Louisiana Monday-night classic — smoky, hearty, and even better reheated the next day.

INGREDIENTS
• 1 pounds dried red kidney beans (or 2 cans, drained)
• 12 ounces andouille sausage, sliced into rounds
• 1 pieces yellow onion, diced
• 1 pieces green bell pepper, diced
• 2 pieces celery stalks, diced
• 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced
• 4 cups chicken broth
• 2 pieces bay leaves
• 1.5 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
• 1 teaspoons smoked paprika
• 0.5 teaspoons dried thyme
• 1  salt and pepper to taste
• 2 cups long-grain white rice
• 3 pieces green onions for garnish

STEPS
1. Soak beans (if dried): If using dried beans, soak 1 pounds dried red kidney beans (or 2 cans, drained) in water overnight or do a quick soak: cover with water, boil 2 min, then let sit 1 hour. Drain. Skip if using canned beans.
2. Brown the sausage: In a large heavy pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, brown 12 ounces andouille sausage, sliced into rounds until slightly crispy, about 3–4 min. Remove and set aside, leaving the drippings in the pot.
3. Sauté the trinity: Add 1 pieces yellow onion, diced, 1 pieces green bell pepper, diced, and 2 pieces celery stalks, diced to the pot. Cook over medium heat until softened, about 5 min. Add 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced and cook 1 more minute.
4. Simmer the beans: Add beans, browned sausage, 4 cups chicken broth, 2 pieces bay leaves, 1.5 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, 1 teaspoons smoked paprika, and 0.5 teaspoons dried thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 1.5–2 hours (or 45 min for canned beans), stirring occasionally, until beans are very tender and creamy.
5. Smash and season: Remove bay leaves. Use the back of a spoon to smash about a quarter of the beans against the pot — this thickens the sauce naturally. Season with 1  salt and pepper to taste.
6. Cook the rice: Cook 2 cups long-grain white rice according to package directions. Serve beans over rice, topped with sliced 3 pieces green onions for garnish.

NOTES
Reheats beautifully — add a splash of broth if it thickens too much. Toddler tip: the sausage slices are easy for little hands to pick up. Freeze leftovers in portions for future lunches.
