Vietnamese Pork Bowls
A quick caramelized pork bowl with fish sauce and brown sugar, served over rice with fresh toppings.

INGREDIENTS
• 1.5 tablespoons cooking oil (peanut oil preferred)
• 0.5 onion, finely diced (brown, white, or yellow)
• 2 teaspoons ginger, minced
• 2 garlic cloves, minced
• 1 bird's eye or Thai chili, deseeded and finely chopped
• 1 pound ground pork
• 5 tablespoons brown sugar
• 2 tablespoons fish sauce

SERVING
• 1 green onion stem, finely sliced
• rice
• sliced red chilli
• tomato
• cucumber

STEPS

1. Heat cooking oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook until softened.
2. Add ginger, garlic, and chili. Cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add ground pork and break it up. Cook until browned and cooked through.
4. Add brown sugar and fish sauce. Stir to combine and cook until the pork is caramelized and the sauce has thickened.
5. Serve over rice, topped with sliced green onion, red chilli, tomato, and cucumber.

NOTES
You can use jar paste for the ginger, garlic, and chili, but add them after the pork is cooked — jar stuff is wet and will cause severe spittage if you try to saute. Use 2 tsp.
