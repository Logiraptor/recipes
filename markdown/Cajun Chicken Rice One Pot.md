Cajun Chicken and Rice (One-Pot)
Everything cooks in one pot — bold Cajun flavor, tender chicken, and rice that soaks up every bit of the sauce.

Servings: 4

INGREDIENTS
• 1.5 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
• 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, divided
• 2 tablespoons olive oil
• 1 pieces yellow onion, diced
• 1 pieces green bell pepper, diced
• 2 pieces celery stalks, diced
• 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced
• 1 cups long-grain white rice, rinsed
• 1 pieces can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
• 2 cups chicken broth
• 1 teaspoons smoked paprika
• 0.5 teaspoons dried thyme
• 3 pieces green onions, sliced

STEPS
1. Season and brown chicken: Toss 1.5 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces with 1 tsp of 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, divided. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown chicken in a single layer, about 3–4 min per side. Remove and set aside.
2. Cook the trinity: In the same pan, add 1 pieces yellow onion, diced, 1 pieces green bell pepper, diced, and 2 pieces celery stalks, diced. Cook over medium heat until softened, about 5 min. Add 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced and cook 1 min more.
3. Add rice and liquids: Stir in 1 cups long-grain white rice, rinsed, 1 pieces can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz), 2 cups chicken broth, 1 teaspoons smoked paprika, 0.5 teaspoons dried thyme, and the remaining 1 tsp 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, divided. Return chicken to the pan. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
4. Simmer covered: Reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and cook for 20–22 minutes until rice is cooked through and liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let sit, covered, for 5 min.
5. Finish and serve: Fluff the rice with a fork, taste and adjust seasoning, then top with 3 pieces green onions, sliced.

NOTES
Reheats well with a splash of broth stirred in. For a milder version for the toddler, serve their portion first before adding extra seasoning. Swap chicken thighs for breast if preferred.
