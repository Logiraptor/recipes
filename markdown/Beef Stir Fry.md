Garlic Ginger Beef Stir Fry with Rice
A quick, high-protein weeknight stir fry with garlic, ginger, and a savory sauce — on the table in under 25 minutes.

INGREDIENTS
• 1.5 pounds flank steak or sirloin, thinly sliced
• 2 cups broccoli florets
• 1 pieces red bell pepper, sliced
• 1 pieces carrot, thinly sliced
• 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced
• 1 tablespoons fresh ginger, grated
• 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
• 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
• 1 tablespoons oyster sauce
• 1 teaspoons sesame oil
• 1 teaspoons cornstarch
• 0.3 cups water
• 2 cups long-grain white rice

STEPS
1. Make sauce and cook rice: Whisk together 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce, 1 tablespoons oyster sauce, 1 teaspoons sesame oil, 1 teaspoons cornstarch, and 0.3 cups water in a small bowl. Set aside. Cook 2 cups long-grain white rice according to package directions.
2. Sear the beef: Heat 2 tbsp 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided in a large skillet or wok over very high heat. Add 1.5 pounds flank steak or sirloin, thinly sliced in a single layer. Sear without moving for 1–2 min, then stir-fry another 1–2 min until browned. Remove and set aside.
3. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add remaining 1 tbsp 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided to the pan. Add 1 pieces carrot, thinly sliced first, stir-fry 1 min. Add 2 cups broccoli florets and 1 pieces red bell pepper, sliced, stir-fry 2–3 min until tender-crisp. Add 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced and 1 tablespoons fresh ginger, grated, cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Combine and finish: Return beef to the pan. Pour sauce over everything and toss to coat. Cook 1–2 min until sauce thickens and coats everything evenly.
5. Serve: Serve immediately over rice.

NOTES
High heat is the key to stir fry — don't overcrowd the pan or it will steam instead of sear. Toddler tip: the broccoli, carrot, and bell pepper are mild and easy to eat in small pieces. Cut veggies into small pieces for little ones. Reheats well the next day.
