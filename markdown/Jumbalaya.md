Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya
The ultimate Southern one-pot meal — smoky sausage, shrimp, chicken, and rice all simmered together with bold Cajun spice.

Servings: 6

INGREDIENTS
• 8 ounces andouille sausage, sliced
• 1 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, diced
• 1 pounds large shrimp, peeled and deveined
• 1 pieces yellow onion, diced
• 1 pieces green bell pepper, diced
• 2 pieces celery stalks, diced
• 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced
• 1 pieces can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
• 2.5 cups chicken broth
• 1.5 cups long-grain white rice, rinsed
• 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
• 1 teaspoons smoked paprika
• 2 tablespoons olive oil
• 3 pieces green onions for garnish

STEPS
1. Brown the meats: Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown 8 ounces andouille sausage, sliced until slightly crispy, 3–4 min. Remove and set aside. Season 1 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs, diced with half the 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning and brown in the same pot, 4–5 min. Remove and set aside.
2. Sauté the trinity: Add 1 pieces yellow onion, diced, 1 pieces green bell pepper, diced, and 2 pieces celery stalks, diced to the pot. Cook over medium heat until softened, about 5 min. Add 4 pieces garlic cloves, minced and cook 1 min.
3. Build the jambalaya: Stir in 1 pieces can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz), 2.5 cups chicken broth, 1.5 cups long-grain white rice, rinsed, remaining 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning, and 1 teaspoons smoked paprika. Return sausage and chicken to the pot. Bring to a boil.
4. Simmer covered: Reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and cook 20–22 min until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed.
5. Add the shrimp: Stir in 1 pounds large shrimp, peeled and deveined, cover, and cook 3–5 min until shrimp are pink and curled. Remove from heat.
6. Serve: Taste and adjust seasoning. Garnish with 3 pieces green onions for garnish and serve directly from the pot.

NOTES
Jambalaya reheats extremely well — the rice continues to absorb flavors overnight. Add the shrimp last so it doesn't overcook. Toddler tip: serve them the milder pieces (chicken and rice) before adding extra seasoning.
