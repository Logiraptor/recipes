Wild salmon & avocado Mediterranean bowl
An omega-3 powerhouse that needs zero cooking. Bright, briny, and deeply satisfying — ready in under 10 minutes.

INGREDIENTS
• 2 ounces wild salmon (canned or 4 oz cooked fillet per person)
• 1 avocado, diced
• 1 cups romaine lettuce, chopped
• 1 cups arugula
• 1 cups cucumber, diced
• 1 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
• 0.5 cups kalamata olives, halved
• 0.5 cups canned artichoke hearts, chopped
• 0.3 cups red onion, finely minced
• 2 tablespoons capers
• 3 tablespoons flat-leaf parsley, chopped
• 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
• 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 1 lemon)
• 1 teaspoons Dijon mustard
• 0.5 teaspoons dried oregano
• 1 pinch salt and black pepper

STEPS
1. Make the dressing: Whisk together 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 1 lemon), 1 teaspoons Dijon mustard, and 0.5 teaspoons dried oregano in a small bowl or jar. Season with 1 pinch salt and black pepper. Set aside — this keeps 5 days in the fridge.
2. Build the salad base: In a large bowl, combine 1 cups romaine lettuce, chopped and 1 cups arugula. Add 1 cups cucumber, diced, 1 cups cherry tomatoes, halved, 0.5 cups kalamata olives, halved, 0.5 cups canned artichoke hearts, chopped, 0.3 cups red onion, finely minced, 2 tablespoons capers, and 3 tablespoons flat-leaf parsley, chopped. Toss to distribute evenly.
3. Add salmon: Flake 2 ounces wild salmon (canned or 4 oz cooked fillet per person) over the salad. If using canned, drain well and pat dry. If using a cooked fillet, break into large chunks.
4. Finish and dress: Add 1 avocado, diced last to keep it from browning. Drizzle dressing over the top and toss gently so the salmon and avocado stay in pieces. Serve immediately.

NOTES
Meal prep tip: store all components (except avocado) in the fridge for up to 4 days. Keep dressing in a separate jar and add avocado only when serving. Boost it into a full meal by adding ½ cup cooked quinoa or farro per bowl.
