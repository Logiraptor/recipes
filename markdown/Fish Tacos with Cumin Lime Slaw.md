Fish Tacos with Cumin Lime Slaw
Quick skillet cod tacos with a creamy cumin-lime cabbage slaw, avocado, and warm tortillas. Works great with frozen cod. Source: budgetbytes.com (4.92 stars, 36 votes).

Servings: 4
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes

INGREDIENTS
• 0.25 cup mayonnaise
• 1 lime, juiced (about 1 tablespoon)
• 0.5 teaspoon ground cumin
• 0.125 teaspoon cayenne pepper
• 0.25 teaspoon salt
• 0.5 teaspoon granulated sugar
• 4 cups coleslaw mix (shredded cabbage and carrots)
• 2 green onions, sliced
• 8 small corn tortillas
• 2 teaspoons chili powder
• 0.5 teaspoon ground cumin
• 0.25 teaspoon garlic powder
• 0.125 teaspoon salt
• 1 tablespoon cooking oil, divided
• 1 pound cod fillets
• 1 avocado, sliced
• 1 jalapeño, sliced, optional

STEPS
1. If using frozen cod, thaw in the fridge overnight or in a sealed bag under cold water, then pat dry.
2. Make the slaw dressing: stir together the mayonnaise, lime juice, 0.5 teaspoon cumin, cayenne, 0.25 teaspoon salt, and sugar.
3. Combine the coleslaw mix and green onions in a bowl, pour the dressing over, and stir to coat. Refrigerate until serving.
4. Toast the tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat until lightly browned on each side. Stack and cover with foil to keep warm.
5. Mix the fish seasoning: chili powder, 0.5 teaspoon cumin, garlic powder, and 0.125 teaspoon salt. Cut the cod into taco-sized pieces and season both sides.
6. Heat 0.5 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook half the fish in a single layer, a few minutes per side, until opaque, firm, and flaking (145°F). Repeat with the remaining oil and fish.
7. Build each taco with fish, avocado, a scoop of slaw, and jalapeño slices if using. Serve warm.

NOTES
Omit the jalapeño and reduce the cayenne for a toddler-friendly version. Cod is mild and flakes easily, making it kid-friendly. Cook the fish in batches so it sears rather than steams.
