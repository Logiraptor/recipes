Instant Pot Butter Chicken
Restaurant-quality Indian butter chicken in under 30 minutes. Creamy, authentic, and easy.

Servings: 4
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 25 minutes

INGREDIENTS
• 1 14-ounce can crushed tomatoes
• 5 cloves garlic
• 1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
• 1 teaspoon turmeric
• 0.5 teaspoons cayenne pepper
• 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
• 1 teaspoon kosher salt
• 1 teaspoon garam masala
• 1 teaspoon ground cumin
• 1 pound boneless skinless chicken thighs
• 4 ounces butter, cut into cubes
• 4 ounces heavy cream
• 1 teaspoon garam masala (for finishing)
• 0.25 cups chopped cilantro

STEPS
1. Combine sauce and cook: Place tomatoes, garlic, ginger, turmeric, cayenne, smoked paprika, salt, 1 teaspoon garam masala, and cumin in the Instant Pot. Mix well. Place chicken thighs on top of the sauce.
2. Pressure cook: Close the lid and cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. Let pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then release any remaining pressure.
3. Remove chicken: Carefully remove the chicken and set aside.
4. Blend sauce: Using an immersion blender, blend the sauce until smooth.
5. Finish the sauce: Let the sauce cool slightly. Add butter, heavy cream, cilantro, and 1 teaspoon garam masala. Stir until well incorporated.
6. Combine and serve: Reserve half the sauce for future use. Add the chicken back in and heat through. Break into smaller pieces but don't shred. Serve over rice or zucchini noodles.

NOTES
No added water is needed — the chicken and tomatoes release plenty of liquid. Let sauce cool slightly before adding butter and cream to keep it thick. If it gets thin, refrigerate briefly to thicken. Don't re-pressure cook the finished sauce. Homemade garam masala makes a huge difference. Use coconut oil and full-fat coconut milk for a dairy-free version. Frozen chicken works — add 1-2 minutes to cook time. Leftover sauce freezes well or keeps 2-3 days in the fridge.
